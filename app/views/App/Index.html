Robots

Additionally, I've been building several robots for competition - Sandstorm, Sideslip, Faultline, Shearzone, and Typhoon.

Sandstorm and Sideslip

Sandstorm and Sideslip will both compete in the 3lb weight class. They're both built atop the same chassis/drivetrain design, but differ in the specific modules and drivetrain configurations installed. I've designed and machined these chassis myself using Autodesk Inventor and a Haas Mini Mill.
This will be the second version of Sandstorm. While it has seen decent competitive success in the past, I'm completely revamping the design to reflect many lessons learned.
Sideslip's primary objective was to serve as a testbed for an experimental drive configuration never before seen in the sport.

Sandstorm 2.0 went 1-2 at it's event in Pomona.

Faultline and Shearzone

Faultline is a fairly last minute addition to the fleet. It's designed to compete in the 1lb Plastic weight class. This weight class is interesting because any structural or damaging components must be made of ABS or PLA plastics. The chassis of Faultline will be 3D printed out of ABS. It was a fairly quick design cycle since it took a lot of design cues from all of my past robots, specifically the first iteration of Sandstorm.
Shearzone was an even faster design/assembly cycle. I was challenged to design a plastic antweight robot in 1 hour. Video below.



Faultline went 3-7 at it's two events thus far, finishing 1st most recently at Rocky Mountain Gear Jam.

Shearzone went 0-3 and has been retired from competition.

Typhoon

Typhoon was a fairly low budget and quick entry into the 2019 LAFTC Coaches' League event designed to play the 2012-13 FTC game. It was designed as a module mounted atop the Sideslip/Sandstorm frame. Read more about it here.
