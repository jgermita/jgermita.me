About me

I like building cool things. I've worked on projects of many different scales and breadths ranging from combat robots to DJ controllers to webapps. Current Projects

Currently, I am working on building out this new site. It replaces an old site I've maintained since 2017. While perfectly adequate, I've always wanted to dive into developing web applications and a complete overhaul of my personal site was the perfect opportunity to begin. This site was built using Go and the Revel framework and deployed onto and hosted by Heroku.
Read more about it here!

Additionally, I've been building 3 combat robots for a competition at the end of July - Sandstorm, Sideslip, and Faultline.
Sandstorm and Sideslip will both compete in the 3lb weight class. They're both built atop the same chassis/drivetrain design, but differ in the specific modules and drivetrain configurations installed. I've designed and machined these chassis myself using Autodesk Inventor and a Haas Mini Mill.
This will be the second version of Sandstorm. While it has seen decent competitive success in the past, I'm completely revamping the design to reflect many lessons learned. You can read more about Sandstorm's past exploits here and here.
Sideslip's primary objective was to serve as a testbed for an experimental drive configuration never before seen in the sport.
Faultline is a fairly last minute addition to the fleet. It's designed to compete in the 1lb Plastic weight class. This weight class is interesting because any structural or damaging components must be made of ABS or PLA plastics. The chassis of Faultline will be 3D printed out of ABS. It was a fairly quick design cycle since it took a lot of design cues from all of my past robots, specifically the first iteration of Sandstorm.
